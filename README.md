## My Background
I have 26+ years of experience with computer programming & software development as well as 16+ years of experience working with supervised machine learning (ML), surrogate modeling, uncertainty quantification (UQ), optimization, reliability analysis, reliability-based design optimization (RBDO), verification and validation (V&V) of simulation models.  I have co-authored 20+ papers related to supervised machine learning (surrogate modeling), uncertainty quantification, reliability analysis and reliability-based design optimization across a wide range of engineering disciplines. I served on international NATO committees working together with global team members to help develop the new standards for military ground vehicle mobility. I have given presentations at conferences and to both technical and non-technical stakeholders of all levels.

I received my Ph.D. from the University of Iowa in 2014, where my research focused on creating a supervised machine learning methodology for noisy responses. The method was built using Bayesian methods to construct Gaussian Process (Kriging) predictive models used for uncertainty quantification and optimization.

After finishing my Ph.D. I co-founded the company RAMDO Solutions. There is more on that in the [RAMDO Solutions](https://github.com/AIDataDr/RAMDO-Solutions) part of my portfolio. As a co-founder of a small business, I worked in every role of a business, even taking out the trash and cleaning the office. I made mistakes along the way, but I learned a lot as well!

While at RAMDO Solutions I developed software that could be used for data analysis and data science work. I developed code and software for carrying out data tasks such as data aggregation, data cleaning, data visualization, exploratory data analysis, design of experiments, and fitting data to distributions. I also developed software that could be used to build supervised machine learning models and use those models to carry out optimization and optimization under uncertainty. I worked on consulting projects with customers to learn about their problems and help them solve them. All these problems were data science type problems requiring data aggregation, data cleaning, data visualization, exploratory data analysis, building predictive models, etc.

## My Next Journey
After exiting RAMDO Solutions it took me a bit to come down off the high of building and running a company and to think about what my skill set is, and what I want to do next. I came to the realization that most all the work I did at RAMDO Solutions for almost 11 years was the same kind of work a data scientist does. It turns out I was a data scientist all those years and never knew it. Of course I did a lot of other work, but the work I really enjoyed was the data science related work on solving complex problems, analyzing data, and building predictive models. I have an entrepreneurial spirit. I like to take ownership of my work and am not afraid of working hard and doing what it takes to solve a problem.

I am looking for a data science type of position. Exact titles are not really that important to me. It’s more about the type of work I do. I want to do something I enjoy. I enjoy programming and working with large datasets, analyzing data, and building machine learning models to solve complex problems. I want the work I do to have some kind of impact that helps people in one way or another, whatever that may mean. I am looking forward to the next journey.

I am interested in a lot of different areas, so I am looking for opportunities in a lot of different industries. Some of the areas I am interested in are:

- Medicine
- Renewable Energy
- Weather
- Financial and Insurance Data
- Risk Management &ndash; Actuary
- Investing &ndash; Trading &ndash; The Stock Market

If you have any ideas, open positions, are interested in learning more, or would like to discuss please reach out to me on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/nicholas-gaul-457515a/). Thank you.
